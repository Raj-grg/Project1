Drama and Puppetry

Introduction

Drama and puppetry are fun and creative ways for young children to express themselves, explore feelings, and tell stories. Through role-play, acting, and using puppets, children build confidence, develop language skills, and learn to work with others. These activities spark imagination and support learning in a playful, engaging way.

Creativity Theories and Perspectives

Drama and puppetry are powerful tools in early childhood learning, supported by well-known educational theories. Vygotsky believed that children learn best through social play, like pretending and storytelling. These activities help them understand the world around them and build thinking skills (Anders, 2021).

Piaget also said that children learn by doing. Through dramatic play, they can explore ideas, try out different roles, and make sense of real-life situations (Howard & Mayesky, 2022).

Tombak (2014) adds that drama helps boost creativity. It gives children the chance to share their thoughts, explore different viewpoints, and express themselves freely. Overall, drama and puppetry help children grow emotionally, socially, and intellectually by making learning active and fun.

Using Materials, Tools, and Technology

To make drama and puppetry more engaging, educators can use a variety of tools and materials. Items like costumes, masks, puppets, and story props help children step into different roles and express their ideas in creative ways. These hands-on resources encourage imagination and make pretend play more meaningful.

Alongside traditional props, digital tools can also enhance learning. Technology such as smartboards, storytelling apps, and video cameras allows children to watch themselves perform and think about how they can improve (Mayesky, 2015). These resources create exciting and interactive learning spaces where children are free to explore, reflect, and bring their creative ideas to life.
